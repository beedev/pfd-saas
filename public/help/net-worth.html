Net Worth

The home dashboard is your one-screen answer to “what am I worth today?” — a single live number on top, every asset class broken out below it, and your debts subtracted to leave the figure that actually matters.

Open Net Worth →

The number on top

The big Total Net Worth tile is the headline. It is computed every time the page loads, from current values — not from a figure you typed in once and forgot:

Net Worth  =  Total Assets  −  Liabilities

Under the headline you'll see a one-line breakdown such as ₹1,42,00,000 assets · ₹38,00,000 liabilities. Click it to expand a panel showing your top assets (largest first) and an itemised list of every liability with a running total — so you can always see exactly where the number comes from.

A second line shows your unrealised gain/loss — how much your market-priced holdings (stocks, mutual funds, gold) are up or down versus what you paid for them.

The unrealised gain line only covers stocks, mutual funds and gold — the holdings that have a live market price and a known cost. Balances like EPF, NPS or a property valuation don't have a “purchase price vs today” in the same sense, so they're not folded into that percentage.

What counts as an asset

Each asset class gets its own tile in the grid. These all add to your net worth:

TileWhat it values
StocksLive market value of your equity holdings
Mutual FundsUnits × latest NAV
GoldGrams × purity × today's gold rate (SGB/ETF/physical/digital)
NPSCurrent Tier-I/II account value
Provident Fund (EPF)EPF/PPF/VPF balance
Small SavingsSchemes like NSC, KVP, SSY, post-office deposits
Real EstateCurrent notional valuation of each property
Insurance (cash)Cash/investment value of Whole Life, Endowment and ULIP policies only
Chit FundsNet amount currently contributed
Fixed DepositsPrincipal locked in active FDs
Forex DepositsForeign-currency balance × today's exchange rate

Term and health insurance are not assets. Only policies that build a cash value — Whole Life, Endowment and ULIP — contribute to net worth. A pure term plan has a death benefit but no surrender value, so it correctly counts as ₹0 here. See Insurance.

What gets subtracted

Liabilities — every loan (home, car, personal) plus outstanding credit-card balances — are subtracted from your assets. They appear in their own rose-coloured tile and in the expandable breakdown so you can see what's dragging the number down. Pay a loan down and the figure rises on the next refresh. Manage them under Liabilities.

Everything is live

Stocks, mutual-fund NAVs, gold and forex all refresh from market sources when the dashboard loads, so the total reflects today's prices, not last month's. Balances you maintain by hand (EPF, property valuations, FDs) show whatever you last saved — keep them current for an accurate total.

A note on conservative valuations

Some tiles are intentionally cautious. Active fixed deposits are shown at principal (accrued interest is projected separately under Planning, not booked here), and chit funds show your net contribution rather than an optimistic prize estimate. Forex rows where today's exchange rate couldn't be fetched are left out of the tile rather than silently valued at zero — open that deposit's detail page and you'll see a “rate unavailable” note.

Snapshots & the 30-day trend

A net-worth figure is most useful as a trend. Use the Capture today's snapshot button (top-right) to record today's totals into a time series.

  1. Click Capture today's snapshot. A “Last snapshot: today” tick confirms it.
  2. Come back over the following days and capture again to build history.
  3. Once you have a few, a 30-day net worth trend sparkline appears near the top.
  4. Already snapshotted today? The button becomes Recapture snapshot — it overwrites today's entry with current values, so it's safe to re-run after adding a holding.

Snapshots do double duty: they also feed the Daily Digest's day-over-day “your portfolio moved ₹X” line. A single daily snapshot (the morning digest job can take one for you) keeps both the trend chart and the digest meaningful.

Allocation & top movers

Below the tiles, two cards add colour to the raw numbers:

  • Asset allocation — a bar per class showing what slice of your assets each one represents. A quick gut-check on whether you're over-concentrated (e.g. 80% in one property).
  • Top movers — today's best and worst performing stocks by percentage, so you can spot what moved your number without opening each holding.

Starting from empty

A brand-new account shows two cards instead of zeros:

  • Explore with sample data — loads a realistic INR portfolio (salary, stocks, funds, insurance, a home loan) so you can click through every screen in seconds. Wipe it later from Settings.
  • Enter my own data — jumps you to income to start with your salary, then add holdings at your own pace.

On a self-hosted instance with the Demo/Personal account switcher turned on, the “Load sample data” card is hidden on purpose — switch to the Demo account from the sidebar instead, so your Personal account stays clean.
