Insurance

Insurance is your protection layer — and some of it is also an asset. Artha tracks three kinds: Life, Health, and Vehicles — keeping every premium due date, sum insured, and renewal in one place so nothing lapses.

The Insurance section in the sidebar opens onto the three policy types. Each has its own page; together they answer two questions — "am I covered enough?" and "what's due next?"

Life Insurance → Health Insurance → Vehicles →

Life Insurance

The Life Insurance page holds your LIC and private policies. It supports four policy types, and the type matters because it decides whether the policy is only protection or also a chunk of your net worth:

TypeWhat it isCounts to net worth?
TermPure protection — pays out only on death, no maturity value.No
EndowmentProtection + guaranteed savings that mature.Yes (cash/maturity value)
Whole LifeLifelong cover, often with a cash/annuity value.Yes (cash value)
ULIPInsurance bundled with market-linked investment.Yes (fund value)

Term policies don't count toward net worth — they have no surrender or maturity value while you're alive. The cash/investment value of Whole Life, Endowment, and ULIP policies does count, since you could surrender them for money today.

Premiums & the premium calendar

For each policy you record the premium amount and frequency (monthly, quarterly, half-yearly, yearly). From the start date (or the next-due date) the app builds a rolling premium calendar of upcoming due dates — so you can see at a glance what's payable in the next 90 days. The page also totals your annualised premium across all policies.

"Mark paid"

  1. Pay your premium with your insurer as usual.
  2. On the policy, hit Mark paid.
  3. The app records the payment date and advances the next due date by the policy's frequency — so the calendar always points at the next instalment.

Marking premiums paid keeps the premium calendar honest and feeds your due-date alerts, so you get a nudge before a policy lapses.

Coverage adequacy

An expandable coverage-adequacy check flags whether your protection is thin — for example, life cover under a rule-of-thumb threshold. Treat it as a prompt to review, not gospel: the right number depends on your income, dependents, and liabilities.

Annuity / pension fields

For Whole Life and pension-style policies, extra annuity fields capture the income the policy is expected to pay out. These feed your retirement projections as a future income stream.

Bulk-import from an LIC statement

Got a stack of LIC policies? Upload your LIC "Premium Paid Statement" PDF and the app extracts every policy at once — policy numbers, sums assured, premiums, and dates — rather than entering each by hand. Policies are matched by policy number, so re-importing updates existing entries instead of duplicating them.

After a bulk import, glance over the policy types — the app's best guess from a PDF isn't always perfect, and the type drives whether a policy lands in your net worth.

Health Insurance

The Health Insurance page tracks your mediclaim policies — individual or family-floater. For each you record the insurer, sum insured, premium, and renewal date. The page surfaces policies coming up for renewal so cover never lapses (a lapse can reset waiting periods).

  • Claims — log claims against a policy to keep a running history; pending claims are highlighted.
  • Portability — a policy ported to another insurer can be marked ported out so your records stay accurate.

Health premiums qualify for a Section 80D deduction. Keep the premium amount accurate so the Income Tax module can claim it (higher limits apply for senior-citizen parents).

Vehicle Insurance

The Vehicles page tracks each vehicle (registration number, fuel type, status) along with its insurance and PUC (Pollution Under Control) certificate. The two expiries you care about most:

Tracked itemWhy it matters
Insurance expiryDriving uninsured is illegal and risky — renew before the active policy expires.
PUC expiryA valid PUC certificate is mandatory; an expired one invites fines.

Both expiry dates drive due-date alerts, so you're warned ahead of time rather than discovering a lapse at a checkpoint.

Mark a vehicle SOLD, SCRAPPED, or TRANSFERRED when you move it on — that retires its alerts so you stop getting reminders for a vehicle you no longer own.

How insurance connects to the rest

  • Net Worth — the cash/maturity/fund value of Whole Life, Endowment, and ULIP policies is included; term, health, and vehicle policies are protection only and don't add to assets.
  • Alerts — premium due dates, health renewals, and vehicle insurance/PUC expiries all fire reminders so nothing lapses.
  • Income Tax — life premiums feed Section 80C, health premiums feed Section 80D.
