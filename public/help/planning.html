Planning & Budget

Everything that looks forward — what you spend each month, the one-off events on the horizon, the goals you're saving towards, and how your money becomes a comfortable retirement. This is where your portfolio meets your life plan.

The Planning section of the sidebar gathers seven tools. They build on each other, so it helps to know how they connect:

ToolWhat it answers
SubscriptionsWhat recurring services am I paying for, and what's the monthly drag?
BudgetWhat do I plan to earn and spend each month across categories?
Monthly ExpensesFor this month, what's planned vs actually paid — and what's still pending?
Cashflow EventsWhat one-off inflows are coming (bonus, FD maturity, policy payout)?
GoalsWhat am I saving for — house, car, education — and by when?
ProjectionsAre my goals on track, and how much must I add each month?
RetirementWill my corpus last, and what income will it throw off after I retire?

They share data. Goals pull from your investments and cashflow events. Projections and Retirement use the expected growth rates you set in Settings. Monthly Expenses auto-fills your SIP, chit, and credit-card outflows. Set things up once and the planning tools stay in sync.

Subscriptions

Open Subscriptions to track recurring services — OTT and streaming, software, cloud, AI tools, fitness, news, gaming, memberships. The point isn't just a list: it's seeing your monthly drag, the steady leak that's easy to forget about.

What the page shows

  • Active count — how many subscriptions are currently running.
  • Monthly drag — every subscription normalised to a per-month figure. A ₹12,000/year plan counts as ₹1,000/month; a quarterly plan is divided by three; a lifetime/one-time purchase counts as ₹0.
  • Annual run rate — the same total across a full year.
  • Cancelled this FY (saved) — what you've stopped paying for this financial year.
  • A category breakdown showing each category's share of the drag.

Adding a subscription

  1. Click Add subscription and pick a category card (Streaming, Software, Cloud, Fitness, News, Gaming, AI, Education, Productivity, Other).
  2. Fill in the name, provider, plan name, amount (₹), and billing frequency (Monthly, Quarterly, Semi-annual, Annual, Lifetime).
  3. Set the start date and next renewal date. (Renewal is disabled for Lifetime — there isn't one.) Optionally add a payment method, URL, auto-renew flag, and notes.
  4. Click Save subscription.

Each subscription carries a status: Active (green), Paused (yellow), or Cancelled (grey). On the detail page, Cancel subscription stamps a cancel date and adds it to your "saved this FY" total. To pause instead, open Edit and change the status. Everything is editable inline.

Budget

Open Budget for the big-picture plan: a spreadsheet-style grid with your income and expense categories down the side and six months across the top. Click any cell to type an amount; the table totals everything for you.

How the grid works

  • Click a cell, type, move on. Press Tab to go right, Enter to go down, Esc to cancel. You can type shorthand like 5K or 1.2L and it expands to the full figure.
  • Use + Add Income and + Add Expense to create categories. Hover a category name to reveal a trash icon to delete it.
  • The grid rolls up Total Expenses and a Savings row (carry-forward + income − expenses). Savings shows green when positive, red when negative.
  • Move the six-month window forward or back with the period arrows.

Carry-forward

The top Carry Forward row lets you roll unspent money from one period into the next. It's an editable amount per month, so a surplus you didn't spend becomes opening savings for the following period instead of vanishing from the plan.

Cells are tinted by what's actually happening: green = paid, amber = unpaid, blue = partially paid (with the real amount in a tooltip). That colour comes from the same auto-tracking the Monthly Expenses page uses — read on.

Monthly Expenses

Monthly Expenses zooms into a single month as a checklist of planned vs paid. Use the month selector to step between months. The summary at the top reads "Paid: ₹X / ₹Y (Z%)" across your expense rows.

Each row has four things: Category, Planned (what you budgeted), Actual (what you've paid), and a Status. To record a payment, just click the Actual cell and type the amount — there's no separate payment dialog.

Statuses

BadgeMeaning
● Paid (emerald)Actual covers the planned amount.
● Partial (orange)You've paid something, but less than planned.
● Pending (amber)Nothing paid yet.
—No amount tracked.

For rows you enter by hand, the badge doubles as a one-click toggle — tap it to mark the row paid (sets actual = planned).

Auto-filled outflows

Some rows fill themselves from your real records, so you don't double-enter them:

  • SIP and chit payments carry an auto badge — pulled from your SIP schedule and chit installments.
  • Credit-card spend carries a CC badge.

Auto-tracked rows are read-only in the Actual column — they reflect what actually happened in your accounts. You can still adjust their Planned figure. Only manual rows let you type the Actual amount.

Recurring expenses

Use the Recurring column to template an expense so it appears automatically each period. Click + Set, choose an amount and frequency (Monthly, Every 3 months, Yearly), and optionally an end month. Stop it anytime from the same modal.

Cashflow Events

Cashflow Events is your timeline of money arriving in the future — an insurance/endowment maturity, an annuity or pension, a PPF/SSY/NSC maturity, rental income, salary, business income, an inheritance, or anything you add by hand. These events feed your goal funding and retirement projections, so the more accurate they are, the better your plan.

Re-derive from assets

Most events are generated for you. Click Re-derive from assets and the app walks your portfolio — policies, deposits, NPS, rentals — and creates or refreshes the maturity and income events it can infer. The toast tells you how many were added, refreshed, kept, or removed.

If you edit a derived event, it's promoted to a manual override (shown with a "Manual" badge) and future re-derives won't touch it. That's how you correct or pin a value the app couldn't infer.

Adding an event by hand

  1. Click Add event.
  2. Give it a name and pick a source kind (insurance maturity, annuity, pension, rental, salary, business, inheritance, other, and more).
  3. Choose a frequency — One-time for a single payout, or Monthly/Yearly for an income stream — and set the start date (and end date, or leave it blank for lifelong).
  4. Enter the amount, an optional growth %, and the tax treatment (Tax-free, or Taxable/TDS). Save.

Events show a frequency badge (One-time = amber, Monthly/Yearly = green) and a tax badge (Tax-free = green, Taxable/TDS = amber). If any endowment policies are missing a maturity date, a banner nudges you to fix it in Life Insurance so the timeline stays complete.

Goals

A goal is a target amount by a target date — a house down-payment, a car, education, a wedding, an emergency fund. The list shows how much each goal needs, how much is currently funded, and the funding gap across all active goals.

Creating a goal

  1. Click Add goal and pick a type (Home, Car, Education, Travel, Emergency, Wedding, Business, Other).
  2. Name it and set a target date.
  3. Choose a disbursement type: a single Lumpsum, or a fixed-period / inflation-linked withdrawal (SWP) — the amount field changes to match (total target vs per-year withdrawal).
  4. Set the expected return %/yr and inflation %/yr (sensible defaults are pre-filled), pick a colour, and save.

Funding a goal

On a goal's detail page you connect money to it:

  • Asset mapping — tick which investments back this goal and allocate a percentage of each. Liquidity tags (liquid / semi-liquid / locked) help you avoid pinning a goal to money you can't reach in time.
  • Earmarked inflows — attach specific cashflow events (e.g. an FD maturity) so they count towards this goal.
  • Funding projection — a chart plus a status badge: Fully funded or Shortfall present, with the monthly contribution required from today to close any gap.

Projections

Projections (titled Savings & Goals) is the bird's-eye view across all your goals: a Goal Coverage % that compares the corpus you've earmarked against everything you're targeting. It answers, in one number, "am I on track overall?"

  • Liquid Assets — tick which asset classes you count as available to fund goals. Liquidity badges (liquid / semi-liquid / locked) keep you honest about what you can actually deploy.
  • Future Savings Plan — add a future lump sum or a future monthly amount to model fresh savings on top of today's corpus.
  • Each goal card shows progress, the corpus selected for it, a projected value at the target date, and — when it's short — a "₹X/mo needed" prompt.

The detailed, per-asset-class expected returns (and per-mutual-fund category blending) that drive goal and retirement maths live in Settings → growth assumptions, not on this page. Tune them there once and every projection updates.

Retirement

Retirement is the most complete planner in the app. It works from your real assets: you tick which holdings will fund retirement, set a few assumptions, and it projects your corpus to retirement age and then year-by-year through retirement — including the income each asset throws off and the tax on it.

Retirement assets & assumptions

  • Retirement Assets — choose which NPS, EPF, small-savings, annuity, insurance, and real-estate holdings count. For property you can model sell vs rent; for NPS you set the lumpsum % and annuity yield.
  • Assumptions — current age, retirement age, today's monthly expense, inflation %, pre- and post-retirement return %, and years in retirement. Edit a value and it saves automatically.

What it tells you

  • Headline figures: corpus needed, what your selected corpus grows to, the gap, and the monthly SIP needed to close it.
  • An on-track banner: On track (green), Tight (amber — the required SIP eats over half your likely savings), or Behind (rose).
  • A corpus runway chart showing whether your money lasts the full retirement, or the year it would run out.

Year-by-year table & corpus breakdown

Expand the year-by-year table to see each retirement year's expense need, income from each source (rental, annuity, NPS, insurance ladder), the tax on that income, the net income, and your year-end corpus. Rows tint amber when a year is tight and rose if the corpus is depleted.

The expandable corpus breakdown card shows how each asset class contributes to the projected corpus, two levels deep, so you can see exactly what's driving the number.

The retirement page applies your planning tax brackets to each year's taxable income (rental + annuity + NPS pension) but doesn't edit them. Adjust the slabs in Settings → retirement tax brackets; the default is 0% up to ₹10L, 15% from ₹10L–₹30L, and 25% above.
