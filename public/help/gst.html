GST

The business side of Artha: raise GST sales invoices, track your purchases, keep customers and vendors on file, and assemble GSTR-1 and GSTR-3B summaries — so when it's time to file, your numbers are already in order.

This workspace helps you prepare and organise your GST data. You still file your actual returns on the official GST portal at gst.gov.in.

Setting up your GSTIN

Before raising invoices, enter your GSTIN in Settings. From it, the app auto-extracts your PAN and state — the GSTIN encodes both — so you don't enter them twice and your invoices carry the correct place of supply.

A GSTIN is 15 characters: the first two are the state code, the next ten are your PAN, and the rest are entity/check digits. That's why entering the GSTIN is enough to fill in PAN and state automatically.

Invoice numbering

Invoice numbers are generated from a prefix plus a starting number, per financial year. Set the prefix and the first number you want to use, and the app increments from there for each new invoice — giving you a clean, sequential, GST-compliant series that resets the way you choose each year.

Sales Invoices

Create and manage outward invoices on the Sales Invoices page. Each invoice records the customer (with their GSTIN), line items, applicable GST, and totals. These are your outward supplies and feed directly into GSTR-1.

  1. Open Sales Invoices and start a new invoice.
  2. Pick a customer — their GSTIN and details fill in.
  3. Add line items; GST is applied and totals computed for you.
  4. Save — the invoice takes the next number in your FY series.

Purchases

Record the invoices you receive from suppliers on the Purchases page. These are your inward supplies and the basis for your input tax credit (ITC) — the GST you've already paid that offsets the GST you owe. Each purchase links to a vendor.

GSTR-1 — outward supplies

The GSTR-1 view summarises your outward supplies (sales) for the period, drawn from your sales invoices. It organises the figures the way the return expects, so you can review your outward tax liability before filing it on the portal.

GSTR-3B — summary return

The GSTR-3B view is the summary return — a consolidated picture of outward supplies, input tax credit, and net tax payable for the period. Use it to sanity-check what you'll declare before submitting on the GST portal.

Customers

Keep your buyers on file in Customers, each with their GSTIN and PAN. Saved customers drop straight into new invoices, so you raise sales faster and with the correct tax details every time.

Vendors

Maintain your suppliers in Vendors, with their GSTIN and PAN. Vendors link to purchase invoices and underpin your input tax credit.

Import CSV

Have invoices or master data in a spreadsheet? Use Import CSV to bring them in in bulk rather than entering each row by hand — useful when migrating from another tool or onboarding a backlog.

A typical GST cycle

  1. One-time: enter your GSTIN in Settings and set your invoice prefix and starting number for the FY.
  2. Add your customers and vendors (or import them).
  3. Through the period: raise sales invoices and log purchases as they happen.
  4. At period end: review GSTR-1 and GSTR-3B.
  5. File the actual returns on gst.gov.in.
