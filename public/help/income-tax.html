Income Tax

A complete, end-to-end income-tax workspace: pull your income together, build your Chapter VI-A deductions from real records, compare the old and new regimes, log capital gains, reconcile Form 16 and 26AS, and assemble everything you need to file — all in ₹ and scoped to one financial year at a time.

Important — this is not tax advice. These tools assist your understanding and preparation only. Figures are estimates based on the data you enter and on rules as we understand them. Always verify against the official Income Tax portal at incometax.gov.in and consult a qualified professional before you file.

The Financial Year selector

Every screen in this section is scoped to a single financial year. Use the FY dropdown at the top-right of the page (e.g. FY 2025-26) to switch years. Changing it re-scopes everything you see:

  • Which salary, income, and TDS rows count.
  • Which deductions and donations apply.
  • Which capital-gain sales fall inside the year (1 April → 31 March).
  • Which Form 16 / 26AS uploads are the authoritative source for that year.

A financial year (FY) runs 1 April to 31 March. The assessment year (AY) is the year you file in — FY 2025-26 is assessed in AY 2026-27. The ITR Wizard and forms speak in AY.

Income

Start on the Income page. This is where every rupee you earned in the chosen FY comes together so the rest of the tax engine has something to work with.

What you record here

  • Salary — gross salary, allowances, and exemptions such as HRA.
  • Other income — interest, dividends, rental income, and any other taxable receipts.

Form 16 becomes the source of truth

The moment you upload a Form 16 for the year (see below), the app treats it as the authoritative source for salary and TDS. Your manually keyed figures stay visible for comparison, but the Form 16 numbers drive the tax computation — because that is the document your employer has already reported to the department.

Enter income by hand early in the year for planning, then upload Form 16 once it arrives. The Reconciliation screen will flag any gap between what you booked and what your employer reported.

Old regime vs New regime

The regime comparison card runs your numbers through both tax regimes side by side and tells you which one costs you less — with a clear recommended pick.

  • Old regime — higher slab rates, but you keep your full Chapter VI-A deductions (80C, 80D, HRA, 24(b)…).
  • New regime — lower slab rates and a higher standard deduction, but most Chapter VI-A deductions are switched off. A few (such as employer NPS under 80CCD(2)) still apply.

The recommendation is a numerical comparison only. Your actual best choice can depend on factors the app does not model — confirm before locking it in for filing.

Capital Gains

Log every sale of shares, mutual funds, or property on the Capital Gains (LTCG / STCG) page. Each sale is classified automatically:

TypeMeaning
LTCGLong-Term Capital Gain — held beyond the long-term threshold for that asset class.
STCGShort-Term Capital Gain — held for a shorter period; taxed differently.

Equity LTCG under Section 112A

For listed equity and equity mutual funds, the app applies Section 112A correctly across all your sales in the year — not sale-by-sale:

  1. It nets losses against gains across every equity sale you logged.
  2. It applies a single ₹1.25 lakh exemption to the aggregate (not one exemption per transaction).
  3. It taxes only the balance above that exemption at the 112A rate.

Indexation

Where indexation applies (e.g. certain property and debt gains), the app uses the Cost Inflation Index (CII) to inflate your purchase cost and reduce the taxable gain.

Capital gains feed the ITR Wizard. Logging even one capital-gain sale typically rules out the simplest forms — keep this page up to date.

Form 16

On the Form 16 page, upload the PDF your employer issued. The app parses it and extracts:

  • Salary — gross and taxable salary figures.
  • Exemptions — including HRA.
  • TDS — tax already deducted and deposited by your employer.

Once uploaded, Form 16 becomes the authoritative salary + TDS source for the year, overriding hand-entered figures in the tax computation.

Form 26AS

Upload your Form 26AS on the Form 26AS page to reconcile TDS credits. Form 26AS is the department's consolidated tax-credit statement — it shows every deductor who reported tax against your PAN. Matching it against your records ensures you claim the right credit and nothing is missing.

Reconciliation

The Reconciliation screen puts three columns side by side — your books, Form 16, and Form 26AS — so you can catch mismatches before they become a notice:

  • Salary you booked vs salary on Form 16.
  • TDS you expected vs TDS on Form 16 vs TDS credited in 26AS.
  • Gaps where a deductor reported tax that you have not recorded (or vice-versa).

Reconcile before you build your filing pack. A mismatch here usually means a missing income entry or an employer correction you haven't picked up yet.

80G Donations

Record eligible charitable donations on the 80G Donations page. These flow into your 80G deduction on the Deductions screen and into the filing pack. Keep the donee name, PAN, and receipt details handy — they are required when you claim 80G.

Documents

The Documents page is your tax document locker for the year — a single place to keep proofs, statements, and receipts so they are all together when you file or if you are ever asked to substantiate a claim.

Filing Pack

The Filing Pack bundles everything for the chosen FY into one place to take to filing (or to your accountant): your income summary, computed deductions, the regime recommendation, capital-gains schedule, TDS credits, and supporting documents.

Run Reconciliation and confirm your Capital Gains are complete before generating the pack — it is only as accurate as the data behind it.

ITR Wizard & forms

The ITR Wizard recommends the right ITR form by eligibility for AY 2026-27, based on the income and gains you have entered. It checks the disqualifiers for you — for example:

  • Capital gains or more than one house property disqualify ITR-4.
  • ITR-1 and ITR-4 now allow a small LTCG under Section 112A of up to ₹1.25 lakh — above that you move to a richer form.

Once the wizard points you to a form, jump straight to the matching worksheet:

Import from TaxCalc

Already keep a tax-calculation spreadsheet? Use Import from TaxCalc to pull those figures into the app instead of re-keying them — handy if you maintain your own working file and want it reflected here.

A sensible filing-season workflow

  1. Pick the right FY in the top-right selector.
  2. Make sure Income is complete; upload Form 16.
  3. Upload Form 26AS and run Reconciliation.
  4. Review Deductions and the old-vs-new regime recommendation.
  5. Log all sales in Capital Gains; add donations in 80G.
  6. Let the ITR Wizard pick your form, then fill the worksheet.
  7. Generate the Filing Pack and verify everything on incometax.gov.in before submitting.
