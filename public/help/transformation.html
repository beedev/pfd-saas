Transformation Tracker

A personal, non-finance corner of the app: a 100-day reset for your daily habits, weight, meals, and a short journal. It's off by default — the dashboard stays finance-first until you decide you want it.

The Transformation Tracker is a simple, daily ritual. Each day you tick off the habits you set for yourself, log your weight, jot what you ate, and write a line or two in a journal. Over a hundred days you build a streak and watch the trend lines move. It lives alongside your money tools because the same discipline that grows a portfolio grows a person.

Turning it on

The tracker is hidden until you enable it, so it never clutters the sidebar for people who only want the finance features.

  1. Open Settings and find the Personal modules card — "Optional, non-finance tools. Off by default so the sidebar stays focused."
  2. On the Transformation tracker row, click Enable.
  3. A new Personal section appears in the sidebar with two links — Today and History.

You can Disable it anytime from the same card. Disabling just hides the sidebar links — your plan and logged days are kept, and turning it back on brings everything right back.

Starting your 100-day reset

The first time you open Today you won't have a plan yet, so you'll see a single card:

Start your 100-day reset
Create your plan with starter habits across Morning Routine, Health, Meals, and Night Routine. You can customise everything afterwards.

Click Start your 100-day reset and the app creates a plan named "100 Days Transformation Challenge", starting today, running 100 days, pre-filled with a starter habit template. Nothing here is fixed — the template is just a head start so you're not staring at a blank page.

Editing the plan

Click Edit (top-right of Today) to change the plan itself: its name, start date, day count, your start and goal weight, and optional daily calorie and protein targets. Save with Save plan.

Changing the start date recomputes every day number, so the "Day 7 / 100" counter shifts accordingly. There's one plan per person — this is your single ongoing reset.

The "Today" view

Today is where you log a day. The header shows your plan name and a one-line summary (start date · start → goal weight · calorie · protein targets). Below that:

  • A date picker with a Today button — you can log or review any day, not just today.
  • A Day counter (e.g. 7 / 100), and hints like "before challenge start" or "challenge complete" for out-of-range dates.
  • A Progress bar — how many of the day's tickable items you've completed.
  • A Weight (kg) field; once filled it shows your change from start and how far you are from your goal.
  • If you set targets, Calories and Protein bars (these fill from your meal entries — see below).

The three kinds of items

Your habits are organised into sections, and each item is one of three kinds:

KindHow it worksCounts toward streak?
CheckA simple tick box — done or not done.Yes
Multi-optionA labelled group where you tick one or more of several options (e.g. choose your activity: walking, stretching, weights, gym), with an optional notes field. Counts as done if you tick at least one.Yes
Text / mealA free-text box for meals or notes (e.g. "Morning Meals"). Used for journaling what you ate, and for the nutrition estimate.No

At the bottom is a Journal box — "What happened today, what you noticed, what you want to change." When you're done, click Save day; it saves your weight, journal, all the ticks, and your meal entries together.

Customising your habits

Click Edit to restructure the day. You can rename or delete any section, rename, delete, or convert items between kinds, and add new items — Add checkbox, Add text field, or Add activity group (a multi-option item where you supply the label and a comma-separated list of options). Add section creates a whole new group. Click Done editing when finished.

Meal nutrition estimates

Text/meal items can auto-estimate calories and protein from what you type. After you type a meal and click away, a small badge appears showing "~N cal · N g protein", and those numbers feed the Calories/Protein bars on Today and the averages in History.

The auto-estimate needs an OpenAI key, configured under Settings → AI nutrition estimates (on self-hosted instances; on the hosted edition the operator sets it). Without a key the tracker still works perfectly — your meal text is saved as always, only the automatic calorie/protein estimate is skipped. See Integrations for setup.

The "History" view

History is where the hundred days add up. At the top, four stat cards: days logged, your weight change (start → latest), average calories (vs target), and average protein (vs target).

Streaks

A Current streak and Longest streak card track your momentum. The rule is simple: a streak day = 100% of your checkbox items completed, counted as consecutive complete days back from your latest logged day.

Charts and the habit grid

  • A weight trend line with a dashed goal-weight reference line.
  • A daily calories & protein bar chart, with your calorie target marked.
  • A habit grid heatmap — one cell per day, coloured by completion: grey = not logged, rose = 0%, amber = under 50%, light green = 50–99%, solid green = 100%. Click any logged day to open it back in Today.
  • A daily entries timeline (latest first) with each day's weight, nutrition, completion, meals, and journal — plus an Open button.
