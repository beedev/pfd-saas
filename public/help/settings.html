Help / Settings

Settings

One place to configure your business profile, the tax assumptions that drive your exemption math, the growth rates behind every projection, notifications, and your data — all in Indian rupees.

Open Settings from the sidebar. The page stacks a series of cards top to bottom; each card saves independently with its own button, so you can change just one thing and leave the rest untouched. Two settings live on their own pages — Tax Rates & Rules and FY Close — both covered below.

Open Settings →

Self-host vs hosted. A couple of cards — the Telegram bot token step and the OpenAI key for nutrition estimates — appear only on self-hosted instances, where those secrets live on your own server. On the hosted edition they are managed for you and stay hidden.

Business profile

Your business identity, used across invoices and the GST module. It's split into three sub-cards, all saved together with one Save Business Profile button.

GST registration details

Enter your GSTIN (the 15-character GST number, e.g. 29AABCU9603R1ZM). As you type, the app validates it and shows a green check when it's well-formed. From a valid GSTIN it auto-extracts:

  • PAN — your 10-character PAN, embedded inside the GSTIN.
  • State — derived from the first two digits (the state code).
  • Entity type — inferred from the GSTIN structure.

You don't type PAN or state by hand. Get the GSTIN right and the rest fills itself in. The GSTIN must be exactly 15 characters.

Invoice settings

  • Financial year — the FY this profile applies to (e.g. FY 2026-27). Required.
  • Invoice prefix — prepended to every invoice number. Default INV-.
  • Starting number — the first invoice number to issue. Default 1, minimum 1.

Tax setup

A handful of toggles that drive the OLD-regime exemption math (HRA, 80D ceilings, the 80U bump) and the 80CCD(2) cap under the NEW regime. These rarely change from year to year — set them once and the tax module does the rest. Save with Save tax setup.

ToggleWhat it changes
Metro cityMumbai / Delhi / Kolkata / Chennai qualify for a 50% HRA exemption; non-metro is 40%. On by default.
Senior citizen (60+)Raises your 80D self/family ceiling from ₹25,000 to ₹50,000.
Parents are senior citizensRaises the 80D parents ceiling from ₹25,000 to ₹50,000.
Permanent disability (sec 80U)Adds the 80U deduction — ₹75,000 (Regular, 40–80%) or ₹1.25 lakh (Severe, >80%). Turning this on reveals a severity dropdown.
Family pensionerSec 57(iia) deduction — lesser of ⅓ of pension or ₹15,000 (Old) / ₹25,000 (New).
Govt employeeSets the 80CCD(2) employer-NPS cap to 14% of salary (vs 10% for private-sector employees).

Why it matters. Get these right once and your Income Tax deductions, HRA exemption, and regime comparison all compute with the correct ceilings — no per-return fiddling.

Personal modules

Optional, non-finance tools that stay off by default so the sidebar stays focused on money.

  • Transformation tracker — daily habits, a weight log, a journal, and a calorie/protein checklist for a 100-day reset. Click Enable to add it to the sidebar (or Disable to remove it). See Transformation Tracker.

Notifications & AI estimates

Telegram notifications

Pair your Telegram account to receive your Daily Digest and Alerts directly in chat. The card header shows Connected or Not connected. Click Connect Telegram, then press Start in the chat that opens to confirm the pairing.

Use a fresh, dedicated bot. On a self-hosted instance you'll first paste a bot token from @BotFather (send it /newbot). Always create a new bot just for your dashboard — don't reuse one shared with anything else. Full walkthrough on the Integrations page.

AI nutrition estimates

Optional. Add an OpenAI key and the Transformation tracker will auto-estimate calories and protein from the meals you log. Without a key the tracker still works perfectly — estimates are simply skipped. Setup details are on the Integrations page.

Asset growth assumptions

The expected annual return for each asset class, used by every goal projection, retirement forecast, and corpus estimate. Each row is a percentage you can edit; the change commits when you click away from the box (0–50% allowed).

Set these conservatively. Past performance is no guarantee — a pleasant surprise beats a missed target. The defaults are deliberately cautious.

Mutual-fund sub-rates

The MF — Equity / Debt / Hybrid rates apply only when you've set a fund's category on its detail page. Funds still marked Unknown fall back to the umbrella Mutual Funds rate.

Per-instrument rates (FD, Small Savings, Chit Funds)

For these three classes you get an extra checkbox: use each instrument's actual rate instead of one class rate. Turn it on and projections use each FD's own interest rate, each small-savings scheme's rate, and each chit's own XIRR. Leave it off (the default) and the single conservative class rate applies to every instrument in that class.

Retirement tax brackets

Planning slabs applied to your retirement-year taxable income (rental + annuity + NPS pension). These aren't the real income-tax slabs — they're your own proxy for how much of your retirement income survives tax. Found on the main Settings page (the editable tax rates live separately under Tax Rates & Rules).

Each row is a threshold (the lower bound of the band, in ₹) and a rate (%). The default ladder is:

Taxable incomeRate
Up to ₹10,00,0000%
₹10,00,000 – ₹30,00,00015%
Above ₹30,00,00025%

Rules the editor enforces: the first row is locked at ₹0 (you can't edit or delete it), thresholds must be strictly ascending, and you can have at most 8 brackets. + Add bracket appends a new band; Reset to defaults restores the 0/15/25 ladder; Save brackets commits.

Tax Rates & Rules (/settings/tax-rules)

India's tax parameters change with each Budget. This page lets you keep them current — per financial year, no code change needed. Pick an FY from the selector, edit, and Save changes. Add FY clones the latest year's values as a starting template for a new one.

The editor is organised into five tabs:

TabWhat you edit
Slabs & RegimeOld- and New-regime income-tax bands, plus standard deduction, the Section 87A rebate, and cess.
DeductionsCaps for 80C, 80CCD(1B), 80D, Section 24(b), 80EEA and more.
Capital GainsLTCG/STCG rates and 112A exemption, split around the July 2024 reform cutoff.
SurchargeHigh-income surcharge brackets for each regime.
PresumptiveSections 44AD and 44ADA — presumed-income percentages and turnover limits.

Amounts are entered in rupees. If no saved row exists for a year yet, you'll see the built-in defaults with a reminder to Save changes to persist them.

FY Close (/settings/fy-close)

An end-of-year checklist that walks you through wrapping up a financial year — tax documents, capital gains, insurance audit, and so on. Each item shows a status (Complete / Partial / Pending) and a Lock toggle so you can freeze a category once you're satisfied. A progress bar tracks how far along you are.

  1. Pick the financial year you're closing from the selector.
  2. Work down the checklist; lock each item as it's confirmed.
  3. Click Close FY to advance your business profile to the next year.

Demo data & your own data

Wipe demo data

Loaded the sample portfolio to explore? Wipe demo data removes only the rows that were seeded as demo content — your own real entries are left completely untouched. You'll be asked to confirm, and the app reports how many demo rows it removed.

Data portability

Export everything you've entered as one JSON file, or replace your data from a previously-saved export. The full walkthrough — including the safety warnings around Replace — is on the Data & Backup page.

Replace is destructive. It wipes every row you own and re-imports from the file in a single transaction. Always keep your latest export safe. Read Data & Backup before using it.
