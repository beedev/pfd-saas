Daily Digest

A single morning snapshot of your finances and the markets — what your portfolio is worth, what moved overnight, what's due, and the headlines that matter — all on one screen at /daily-digest.

The Daily Digest is built to be glanced at, not studied. Open it with your coffee and you'll know, in ten seconds, whether anything needs your attention today. The very same digest can land in your Telegram chat every morning automatically — see Integrations.

Open Daily Digest →

Portfolio Snapshot

The top section answers the first question of the day: how much am I worth, and which way did it move?

  • Net Worth — your total across every asset class, in ₹, with the change against the previous snapshot shown beneath it.
  • Day Change — the rupee movement since the last snapshot (often, but not always, yesterday — see the gotcha below).
  • Assets Tracked — how many holdings are feeding the total.

Below those three numbers is a per-asset-class breakdown: one card for each class (stocks, mutual funds, gold, NPS, EPF, real estate, chit funds, and so on), each showing its current value and its rupee change versus the previous snapshot. This is where you spot, at a glance, that equities slipped while gold ticked up.

The day-over-day change is measured against your most recent earlier snapshot, not literally yesterday. If you didn't open the app over a long weekend, the comparison spans that gap — the digest labels it vs <date> so you always know the baseline.

MF movers — Top Gainers & Underperformers

Two compact lists call out your mutual funds that moved the most:

  • Top Gainers — up to three funds with the largest positive change.
  • Underperformers — up to three funds in the red.

Each row shows the fund name and its percentage move, so you can tell whether a dip in the total came from one fund or the whole book.

Action Items

The to-do list of money. The digest scans your records and surfaces anything coming due, grouped by type and ordered with the most urgent first:

  • SIPs Due — systematic investments scheduled to run.
  • Chit Installments Due — upcoming chit-fund payments.
  • Insurance Premiums Due — life, health, and vehicle premiums.
  • Loan EMIs Due — equated monthly instalments.

Each row shows the label, the amount in ₹, and the due date; anything already past its date is tagged with a red Overdue badge. When nothing is pending you'll see a cheerful "You're all caught up!"

Credit-card due dates and a budget summary are also computed and included in the Telegram version of the digest. Want notifications the moment something is actually due, rather than a once-a-day round-up? Set up rules on the Alerts page.

News

Two curated feeds from The Economic Times, refreshed through the day:

  • Market Headlines — the top market stories.
  • Personal Finance — wealth and money headlines.

Each headline links out to the full article with a relative timestamp (e.g. "2h ago").

Taking a snapshot & refreshing

The digest needs a record of today's values to compute the day change. It handles this for you:

  1. When the page loads, it quietly records today's net-worth snapshot so the numbers stay current.
  2. If for some reason today's snapshot is missing, the Portfolio Snapshot shows "No snapshot for today yet" with a Take Snapshot button — click it to record today's values on the spot.
  3. Use Refresh (top-right) any time to re-pull live prices and rebuild the page.

The day-change needs history. The very first time you use the app there's nothing to compare against, so the day-over-day figures stay blank until at least one prior snapshot exists — they'll appear from your second day onward.

If the Economic Times feed is briefly unavailable, the News cards may come up empty and you'll see "No headlines available". That never blocks the rest of the page — your snapshot, market pulse, and action items still load normally.

Get it in Telegram every morning

Once your Telegram account is connected, the digest is formatted and delivered to your chat automatically each morning — no need to open the app at all. Connecting takes a minute; the full walkthrough is on the Integrations page.
