Investments

The Investments section is where your wealth actually lives. This page covers the market-priced classes — stocks, mutual funds, SIPs and gold — plus the bulk PDF importer. Each one updates from live sources so your net worth stays honest.

How every detail page works

Across the whole section the pattern is the same, so once you've learned one you've learned them all:

  • List page — totals at the top, one card or row per holding, an Add button top-right.
  • Detail page — open a holding to see its full breakdown. Hit Edit, change only the fields you want, then Save (or Cancel to discard). You only ever change what you touch.
  • Money is ₹ (INR) everywhere, formatted Indian-style (lakhs/crores).

You don't need a brokerage login. Artha tracks what you hold and prices it live — you enter quantity and cost once, and the app keeps the valuation current.

Stocks

Track Indian equities at live prices.

  1. Open Stocks and click Add stock.
  2. Enter the symbol with its exchange suffix — RELIANCE.NS for NSE, 500325.BO for BSE — and use the lookup to confirm the name and current price.
  3. Enter your quantity and average buy price (your cost per share).
  4. Save. The card now shows current value, your cost, and the profit/loss in ₹ and %.

Live quotes come from Yahoo Finance. Use the right suffix — .NS (NSE) or .BO (BSE) — or the price lookup won't find your stock. Your gainers and losers also surface in Top movers on the home dashboard.

Mutual Funds

Track fund holdings by their official AMFI scheme, valued at the latest NAV.

  1. Open Mutual Funds → Add fund.
  2. Search the AMFI catalogue and pick the exact scheme (e.g. the right Direct/Growth plan) — this locks in the scheme code used to fetch NAV.
  3. Enter the units you hold. Current value = units × latest NAV.
  4. Set the category — Equity, Debt or Hybrid.

The category isn't cosmetic: it drives the growth-rate assumption used in goal and retirement projections. Equity, debt and hybrid funds are projected forward at different expected returns, so categorise honestly for realistic forecasts.

SIPs

A SIP is a recurring monthly investment linked to a fund you already track. Artha can run them for you.

  1. Open SIPs → Add SIP and pick the linked fund.
  2. Set the monthly amount and frequency (monthly is typical) and the start date.
  3. On each due date the SIP auto-executes — it buys units using that day's NAV and adds them to the linked fund, so your unit count grows automatically.

Each SIP shows its XIRR — the annualised return that accounts for the timing of every instalment, which is the right way to judge a staggered investment (a simple average would mislead).

If the app was offline on a due date, the overdue instalment is caught up the next time it runs — using the NAV for the original due date, not today's — so your history stays accurate.

Gold

Track gold across all four forms, valued at the live rate.

  1. Open Gold → Add gold and choose the type: Sovereign Gold Bond (SGB), Gold ETF, Physical, or Digital.
  2. For weight-based forms (SGB / physical / digital) enter grams and purity (999, 995 or 916). For an ETF, enter the symbol and units instead.
  3. Enter what you actually paid as the cost basis.

Use your full purchase cost — including GST and any making/wastage charges on physical gold — as the cost basis. That's the real amount out of your pocket, so your profit/loss reflects reality rather than the bare metal price. Current value is always recomputed from the live rate (grams × purity), never a stale stored figure.

Import from a statement

Rather than typing balances by hand, you can bulk-import from an official PDF.

  1. Open Import from statement.
  2. Upload your PDF (e.g. an EPFO member passbook or an NPS Statement of Transactions). The app auto-detects the format.
  3. Preview what was extracted, then confirm to save it into the right asset class.

Only PDFs are accepted, and the file is parsed on the server — nothing is shared with a third party. Always eyeball the preview before confirming; a scanned or password-protected PDF may not parse cleanly, in which case add the holding manually.
