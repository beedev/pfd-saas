Data & Backup

Your financial data is yours. Export it to a single file whenever you like, restore from a saved export, and understand exactly what is — and isn't — captured.

Everything here lives under Settings → Data portability. There are two actions: Download JSON (export) and Replace from JSON (restore).

Open Settings →

Export — Download JSON

Download JSON bundles everything you've entered — investments, insurance, loans, real estate, chit funds, tax records, budgets, goals, the lot — into a single .json file and saves it to your device. It's a complete, portable snapshot of your account at that moment.

Make it a habit. Download an export before any big change — a bulk import, a replace, or a major edit. It's your personal undo button and takes two seconds.

Restore — Replace from JSON

Replace from JSON takes a previously-saved export and rebuilds your account from it. It wipes every row you own and re-imports from the file — all in a single transaction, so it either fully succeeds or makes no change at all.

  1. Click Replace from JSON… and pick a .json export file.
  2. Review the preview — it shows when the export was taken, how many rows it contains, how many of your current rows will be wiped, and a per-table breakdown of what gets removed and added.
  3. Type REPLACE in the confirmation box to unlock the button.
  4. Click Replace my data. The page reloads with the restored data.

Replace is destructive and cannot be undone from inside the app. It deletes all of your current data first. Only ever replace from an export you trust, and keep a fresh Download JSON on hand before you do. It only ever affects your own account's data — never anyone else's.

If the file contains fields the app no longer recognises, the preview tells you how many will be safely ignored — the import still proceeds with everything it understands.

What's covered — and what isn't

The export captures the structured, parsed data you've entered or imported — the numbers, dates, and records that make up your dashboard.

Included in the exportNot bundled
Investments, SIPs, gold, NPS, EPF, small savings, FDs, forexThe original source PDFs you uploaded (statements, CAS, etc.)
Insurance, liabilities, real estate, chit fundsLive market prices (these are fetched fresh, not stored)
Tax records, deductions, capital gains, budgets, goalsApp configuration that isn't your data
Your settings, assumptions, and profile

About uploaded documents. When you import from a PDF, the app reads the numbers out of it and stores those. The export references such sources by filename — the PDF file itself is not packed into the JSON. Keep your original statements in your own records if you need them later.

Server-side backups (self-host)

Your data lives in the server's database. On a self-hosted instance, the deployment runs an automated periodic backup of that database, so there's a safety net at the infrastructure level independent of anything you do in the app.

Belt and suspenders. The server backup protects against disk or container loss; your own Download JSON protects against mistaken edits and gives you a file you can take anywhere. Use both.

Quick recommendations

  • Export with Download JSON after any session where you added a lot.
  • Always export immediately before using Replace from JSON.
  • Store exports somewhere you control — they contain your full financial picture.
  • Keep your original statement PDFs separately; they aren't inside the export.
