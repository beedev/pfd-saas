Liabilities

Track every loan and credit card in one place — outstanding balances, EMIs, amortization schedules, and the home-loan tax breaks that feed straight into your tax return. Whatever you owe reduces your net worth, so keeping this current keeps your true financial picture honest.

Open Liabilities → Add a liability →

What lives here

The Liabilities page splits into two groups:

  • Loans — home, car/auto, personal, education, or "other" (gold loan, business loan, etc.). Each carries a principal, interest rate, tenure, EMI and a running outstanding balance.
  • Credit cards — revolving credit with a limit, a monthly statement amount, a due date and a utilisation gauge.

At the top you'll see four headline figures: Total Debt (sum of all outstanding balances), Monthly Obligations (all EMIs added up), Total Credit Limit and Average Utilisation across your cards. A Debt health panel turns your monthly outflow into an annualised number and flags card utilisation above 30%.

Why your outstanding matters. Your current outstanding (loans) and current balance (cards) are subtracted from your assets on the Net Worth dashboard. Pay down a loan and update the balance here — your net worth rises automatically.

Adding a loan or card

  1. Click Add liability and pick a type tile — the form adapts to loans vs. cards.
  2. For a loan: enter the lender, principal, current outstanding, interest rate and tenure. The app computes the EMI for you using the standard EMI formula as you type — you don't enter it by hand.
  3. For a card: enter the credit limit, current balance, total & minimum due, and the statement/due days of the month (1–31).
  4. Add the start date (required) and an optional account/loan number, then Save.

Don't know your exact EMI? That's fine — type principal, rate and tenure and let the app calculate it. If your bank's EMI differs slightly (rounding or a rate reset), you can override it later from the loan's detail page, or upload the bank's schedule (below) and tap Sync to bank values.

The EMI calculator & amortization schedule

Open any loan to see its detail page. Below the headline stats you get an amortization schedule — a month-by-month breakdown of how each EMI splits into interest (in red) and principal (in green), with the balance falling to zero. By default the app projects the next 12 months from your current outstanding, rate and EMI.

Upload your bank's real schedule

Computed schedules are an estimate. For the exact figures your lender uses, hit Upload schedule and attach the CSV or PDF amortization sheet from your bank. The app then shows the full real schedule with a Paid / Due status per month, and you can tick each month off as you pay it (which lowers the outstanding balance).

Prepayment simulator

Loans also include a prepayment simulator. Try a one-time or recurring extra payment and instantly see how many months and how much interest you'd save. When a bank schedule is uploaded, the simulator trusts those figures over the manually-entered rate for a more accurate result.

Home-loan tax flags (Section 80C & 24b)

A home loan does double duty at tax time: the principal you repay counts toward your Section 80C deduction, and the interest counts toward Section 24(b). On every loan's detail page, under Tax treatment, two switches control this:

FlagMeaningCap
Principal qualifies for 80CPrincipal repaid this FY is added to your 80C deductions.Within the overall ₹1.5L 80C ceiling.
Interest qualifies for 24(b)Interest paid this FY is added to your house-property interest deduction.₹2L for self-occupied; uncapped for a let-out property.

Both flags are switched on automatically for home loans. For other loan types they're off by default — but you can toggle them (for example, an "other" loan that's really financing a let-out property).

The "already counted" hint

When a flag is on, the page shows a live note like:

FY 2025-26: ₹1,48,200 principal · ₹1,92,640 interest already counted in your tax deductions.

This tells you exactly how much of this loan the app has already fed into your Income Tax deductions for the selected financial year — worked out by walking the amortization schedule across the FY window. It's there so you never double-claim or wonder where a figure on your tax page came from.

One source of truth. You don't re-type these amounts on the tax page — flip the flag here, and the loan's principal & interest flow into Section 80C and 24(b) for the chosen FY automatically.

Credit cards — statements & payments

Cards are tracked statement-by-statement rather than transaction-by-transaction. The aim is to capture what you owe each month and what you actually paid.

  1. Record a statement. On the card (list page Statement button, or the detail page's Monthly Spend History) enter the statement total, statement date and due date. This updates the budget for the due-date month so your Planning & Budget view reflects the bill.
  2. Mark it paid. Use Mark Paid and enter the amount you settled and the date. The card's outstanding balance drops by that amount (floored at ₹0), so a partial payment leaves the remainder outstanding.

A utilisation gauge (balance ÷ limit) shows on each card and turns amber above 30%. Recording the same due-month statement again simply updates it — the app upserts by card and due-month rather than creating duplicates.

Statement vs. paid. The statement amount is what the bank billed; amount paid is what you sent. Track both and a partly-paid card stays visibly outstanding until you clear it — handy for spotting a forgotten balance.

Stay ahead of due dates

Set up rules on the Alerts page to get a nudge before an EMI or a card payment is due — delivered to Telegram or in the Daily Digest. See Alerts for the payment-reminder rule types.

Tips & gotchas

  • Keep balances current. Net worth is only as accurate as your outstanding figures — update after each EMI or, better, mark months paid on the uploaded schedule.
  • Utilisation under 30%. The gauge nudges you here to protect your credit score; high cards are highlighted in amber on the list.
  • Sync preview, always. Sync to bank values shows a full before/after preview — never blindly confirm if the figures look off.
  • Tax flags are FY-scoped. The "already counted" hint follows the financial-year selector logic; switch FY context on the tax pages to see a different year's allocation.
  • Deleting a loan removes its schedule too. The amortization rows and recorded card statements go with it.
