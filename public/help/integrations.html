Integrations

Two optional integrations make Artha come to you instead of the other way around: Telegram delivers your digest and alerts to chat, and an OpenAI key auto-estimates the nutrition of meals in the Transformation tracker. Both are configured in Settings.

Open Settings →

Telegram

This is the one most people want. Connect Telegram and your Daily Digest arrives in chat every morning, and your Alerts ping you the moment they fire. You create a small private bot, tell Artha its token, and pair your account — about two minutes, once.

Set it up

  1. Create a bot. In Telegram, open @BotFather and send /newbot. Give it a name, then pick a username that ends in bot. BotFather replies with a token (a long string like 123456789:ABC…) — copy it.
  2. Save the token. In Artha, go to Settings → "Telegram notifications", paste the token into the bot-token field, and click Save. You'll see a confirmation naming your bot.
  3. Connect your account. Click Connect Telegram. Tap the deep link it opens (or, in Telegram, message your bot with /start <code>), then press Start. The bot replies to confirm, and the page flips to Connected.
  4. You're done. Your daily digest and alerts will now be delivered to that chat.

Use a brand-new, dedicated bot. Create a fresh bot from BotFather just for Artha — do not reuse a bot that's already wired up to something else. A Telegram bot can only have one listener at a time, so a shared bot will fail to pair (or silently break the other tool). One bot, one app.

Each person who connects gets their own private chat with the bot — your digest and alerts only ever go to you. A new user pairs exactly the same way: create their own bot, save its token, and connect.

To stop receiving messages, return to the same card and click Disconnect.

OpenAI key (optional)

This one powers a single nicety: in the Transformation tracker, when you log a meal, an OpenAI key lets the app automatically estimate the meal's calories and protein for you. It's entirely optional.

  1. Get a key from platform.openai.com/api-keys — it starts with sk-.
  2. In Artha, go to Settings → "AI nutrition estimates", paste the key, and click Save.

The tracker works fully without a key — you can log every meal and see all your history. The only thing skipped is the automatic nutrition number, which you can still type in yourself. Behind the scenes it uses OpenAI's inexpensive gpt-4o-mini model, so the cost is typically a few pennies a month.

Where the keys live

On a self-hosted instance, both the Telegram bot token and the OpenAI key are stored on your own server and take effect immediately — no redeploy or restart needed. Save, and the next digest, alert, or meal estimate uses them right away.
