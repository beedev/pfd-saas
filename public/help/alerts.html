Help / Alerts

Alerts

Rules you define, watching your portfolio and the markets, that ping your Telegram chat the moment something happens — a price level breaks, a milestone is hit, or a payment is coming due. Set them up at /alerts.

Where the Daily Digest is a scheduled morning round-up, Alerts are reactive: each rule is a condition, and when it becomes true you get a message. Between scheduled checks (every couple of hours during market hours) you can also fire all your rules on demand.

Telegram is required to actually receive alerts. A rule can trigger, but with no chat connected there's nowhere to deliver the message — so it's skipped quietly until you pair Telegram. Connect it first: see Integrations.

Open Alerts → Connect Telegram →

The three families of rules

Every rule belongs to one of three categories. Add one with the Add Rule button, pick a rule type, and fill in the threshold.

Market

Watch prices and indices. These rules use a symbol (e.g. ^NSEI for Nifty 50, GC=F for gold) and, where relevant, an operator.

Rule typeFires when…Example
Price levelA symbol crosses above / below, or is greater-than / less-than, a level you setGold > ₹16,000 / gram
Index % changeAn index moves by your % threshold in a day (either direction)Nifty moves 3% today
VIX breachIndia VIX reaches your level — a volatility / risk warningIndia VIX ≥ 20

For the Price-level rule you choose how to compare: Crosses above / Crosses below (fires on the edge of crossing, vs the previous close) or a plain greater-than / less-than level.

Portfolio

Rule typeFires when…Example
Net-worth milestoneYour total net worth reaches a ₹ figureNet worth ≥ ₹1 crore
MF weekly dropA mutual fund falls by more than your % threshold (checked per week)Any fund down > 5% this week
Budget overspendSpending in a budget category exceeds the plan by your % thresholdOverspent a category by 20%

Payments

Never miss a due date. These rules look at your records and warn you a chosen number of days before something is due.

Rule typeFires when…
Credit-card dueA card payment is due within N days
Chit installment dueA chit-fund instalment is due within N days
Insurance premium dueA life / health premium is due within N days
Loan EMI dueA loan instalment is due within N days

Vehicle-insurance renewal and PUC-expiry reminders also exist in the alert engine. They run when configured, though they aren't yet offered in the Add-Rule dropdown — your vehicle renewal and PUC dates are tracked under Insurance in the meantime.

What every rule has

  • Name — a label so you recognise it in the list.
  • Category & rule type — what it watches (above).
  • Symbol & operator — for Market rules, the ticker and how to compare.
  • Threshold — the number that triggers it (a price, a %, a ₹ amount, or "days before").
  • Enable toggle — switch a rule off without deleting it. Disabled rules are dimmed and skipped during checks.
  • Cooldown (hours) — the minimum gap before the same rule can notify you again. Default is 24 hours.

The cooldown is your spam shield. Without it, a rule like "Nifty down 3%" could fire on every single check while the condition stays true. A 24-hour cooldown means you hear about it once, then not again until the next day.

Check now

You don't have to wait for the scheduled run. Press Run Check Now and every enabled rule is evaluated immediately against live data. A toast tells you the outcome — how many rules were checked, how many alerts were sent, and how many were de-duplicated.

De-duplication & history

Two safeguards keep alerts useful rather than noisy:

  • De-duplication — within a rule's cooldown window, the same condition won't notify you twice. If you click Run Check Now repeatedly, repeats are counted as "deduped" rather than re-sent.
  • History — every alert that actually fires and is delivered is recorded in the Alert History table (time, rule, category, message, and the value that tripped it), so you have a paper trail of what notified you and when.

If a rule triggers while Telegram isn't connected, the message is skipped and not logged to history — so once you connect Telegram, that same condition can still alert you the next time it's checked.
